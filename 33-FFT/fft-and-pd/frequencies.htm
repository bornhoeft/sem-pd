7. "Frequencies Between"


"Frequencies Between" I call those frequencies, which are no base frequencies. They are not represented by integers and do not "fit" into the FFT-Window. Their phase is changing over time.

In the following case the frequency is somewhat more than eight cycles per window. On the right side you can see the corresponding phase-values. The phase-value measures the difference to the cosine-wave in its initial state. On the one hand you can see the phases shifting slowly, on the other hand you will recognize, that phases of neighbouring channels are rotated by 180° (paraphase). Nevertheless all phases occurring in those channels are moving (nearly) synchronously - indepentent of the size of the amplitude.

The phase shifting of a single steady frequency runs linear. But even at such ideal conditions this linearity is destroyed as soon as a second wave apperas, as because two frequencies close to each other having influence on the same frequency channel. There is no solution for this problem, only compromise-methods. In the simpliest case you assume that the phase of each channel continues linear, as it is done in a phase vocoder.

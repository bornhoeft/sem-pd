3. Base Frequencies

 

The "Base Frequencies" are whole numbers (integer numbers), which means each wave makes that certain number of complete cycles in N samples. Its phaseangle is at the entrance as large as at the exit. The 64th sample has the same phase-value as the sample 0. In the Figures the graphic representation does not show smooth curves, this is because of the digitalisation. In the analogue world they would be smooth sine-waves.

The angles of the phases are important for many considerations concerning the FFT. I will talk about that later...

In the beginning the working in the Frequency Domain is confusing enough (especially for non-mathematicians). Besides the general difficulties in understanding the nature of the Fourier-Transform there are some special problems with interpreting the values for example of angles, which appear to us practically never in value-ranges from 0° to 360°, but rather in ranges of -Pi to +Pi or from -1 to +1. These numbers are radians, a measure for the distance that is covered on the circumference to define the particular angle. the whole circumference is 2*Pi, which is equivalent to 360°. The factor Pi can be omitted, which results in a range of -1 to +1.

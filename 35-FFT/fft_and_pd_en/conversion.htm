4. Conversion Polar to Rectangular View

 

There are two ways to represent frequencies in the frequency domain:

By complex numbers: they represent the cosine- and sine-components of the frequencies. The values x and y are seen as a complex number of the form x + yi.

By amplitude and phase (also a pair of numbers) where the phase usually is expected to appear in radians.(Fig. 8 and 9).
 

Fig. 8
Unit Circle, complex plane
 
 
 

 

Fig.  9
R (the hypothenuse of a right-angled triangle, practically the amplitude)
and the phase-angle Theta define two unique cosine (x) and sine (y)- values.

Depending on what you consider to do, you will have to convert from one format to the other. 

The Amplitude is the (vector)sum of the cosine and sine-components. The phase can be calculated from the arcus tangent of the gradient angle.

In Pd there are three (at least) possibilities to perform these conversions:

Fig. 10
The object "rec2pol~"
 

"rec2pol~" assumes the conversion of complex numbers into amplitudes and phases. The inlets are Real and Imaginary Part, the outlets are phase values (ranging from -Pi to +Pi) at the left side and amplitudes at the right.

Fig. 11
The object "pol2rec~"
 

The other way round phase and amplitude conversion to complex numbers is manged by "pol2rec~". Phasevalues at the inlets have to be between -Pi and +Pi. Both objects are included in the library of Ian Mott (motex). http://www.reverberant.com/Pd

 

Alternatively you can build the conversions yourself with Pd-objects, therefore there is an object "atan2~" from Günter Geiger´s library (ggee). http://gige.xdv.org/pages/pd/pages/download

Fig. 12
"atan2~" from the library of Günter Geiger

But beware, you have to connect the Real Part to the right inlet and the Imag to the left. The output-values range from -0.5 to 0.5. This object also checks the signs of all cases with negative real-part and shows the angles correctly.

Re-converting is tricky, because there is no "sin~"-object. Add 0.75 to the phase angle and you will get the sine.

Fig. 13
Re-Conversion from Amplitude and Phase to Real-Part and Imaginary-Part.

 

And the third way is to use the "expr~"-object from Pd´s "extra"-directory.

 

Fig. 14 and 15
How to use the "expr~" object

Here you can connect the real part to the left inlet, and the imag to the right.
